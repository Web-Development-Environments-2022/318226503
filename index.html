Q&A

3 main differences between web1, web2, web3

1. In web1.0 you could only read the website. In web 2.0 you could read and write- create content and in web3.0 you can read, write and control the website.
2. web 1.0 had millions of users, web 2.0 had billions of users and web 3.0 has trillions of users. the number of users just keep growing tremendously
3. in web 1.0 you could find people with address book, in web 2.0 you could find people with Online social networks, and in web 3.0 you could find people with semantic social networks

searching "side effects of medicine" in web1 and web3

In web 1.0, to search the side effects of a medicine, you had to actively enter sites about medicines and search for that specific information. In web 2.0 you entered the keywords into a search engine, and it runs the words and looked for a match, based on those keywords only- meaning it may return the side effects of other medicines, or will return sited with information about the medicine- but without the side effects. In web 3.0 the search engine will perform a smart search, focusing on semantic models, it will interpret the context of the search and more. In web3.0 you will get a more accurate sites for your query.
